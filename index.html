Introduction

History

The Castle family has owned the Cheboygan home along the shores of Lake Huron since the late 1940's when Thomas Frost bought the double lot between Cheboygan and Mackinaw City shortly after World War II. The lot and the house have changed hands but always remained in the family.

Future

In 2020, Gerry Castle's surviving siblings decided to invest funds from his estate into re-modeling the Cheboygan house. After purchasing the house and property from his parent's estate, Gerry had expressed his plans, on several occasions, to fix up the Cheboygan house and make it a place to where his extended family could return. In honor of his wishes and in memory of Gerry and other family members who are no longer with us, the house will be renovated for the enjoyment of future generations.

House Re-model Project

Multiple Phases

The scope of the project is to re-model every room in the house - only the garage and attic will be left undisturbed. Additionally, a large deck will be extended off the front of the house facing the lake. New plantings will be landscaped around the house, and saplings will be planted throughout the property replacing the many trees that have fallen over the last decade.

Home improvements will be undertaken with a goal of simplifying and extending the maintainability of the home. Another goal of the renovations is to make the house and property more attractive and comfortable as a vacation getaway for our family's next generations.

Renovation and re-modeling work will be broken into distinct phases spanning multiple years. Construction will occur during the late fall and through the winter months, thus leaving the house available for use and enjoyment during the spring, summer, and fall color seasons.

2020-2021 Construction Scope

Construction for the first annual phase of the project began in Late October 2020 and is expected to finish in March 2021. The scope of this year's project is to complete the following:

  • Demo the kitchen, dining room, and front bathroom to unfinished walls
  • Install kitchen doorwall entrance and replace front kitchen window
  • Install new kitchen cabinetry, countertops, sink, backsplashes, engineered wood plank flooring, and interior and exterior lighting
  • Install new bathroom, tub, vanity and sink, tile flooring, lighting, built-in shelves, and pocket door
  • Drywall the dining room and install recessed lighting
  • Finish trim and paint rooms
  • Remediate any plumbing, electrical, or structural issues uncovered in the demolition phases, including to date:
    • New studwalls and insulation in kitchen
    • Re-plumbing of drainline to connect to main house septic line
  • Re-route plumbing along interior walls to prevent pipe freezing
  • Install new electrical sub-panel in utility room and route all new electrical circuits to it.
