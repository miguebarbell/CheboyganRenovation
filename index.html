Introduction

History

The Castle family has owned our Cheboygan home along the shores of the Straits of Mackinac since 1943 when Thomas Frost bought a double lot between Cheboygan and Mackinaw City a mile west of Point Nipigon. A few years later, after the end of World War II, Great Grandpa and Great Grandma Frost built a vacation cabin on the property. The lot and the house have since changed hands four times, but it has always been in the family.

Future

Gerry Castle bought the house and lot in 2015 from his parents’ estate. Gerry had planned to update the house. In several conversations, he had talked about making the Cheboygan property a special place where he could share time with his family and friends. After Gerry passed away in 2018, his surviving siblings decided to re-model the Cheboygan home with funds from Gerry’s estate. In accordance with Gerry’s wishes, and in memory of Gerry and other family members who are no longer with us, the house will be renovated for the use and enjoyment of present and future generations.

House Re-model Vision

Re-model Project Scope

The Cheboygan renovation project will take several years to complete. The scope includes re-modeling each room in the house; extending a new deck off the northwest, front side of the house facing towards the Mackinac Island; and planting native trees, shrubs, and flowers on the property. The new plantings will replace the many trees that have fallen in the past decades and restore the property to a more natural and ecologically sustainable state.

Re-model Goals and Principles

Home improvements will be undertaken with the goal of simplifying and extending the maintainability of the home and the property. A guiding principle of the renovations is to enhance the house’s charm and comfort while retaining its distinguishing features. The updates will be complementary to the house’s past, resulting in a home that would be recognizable to its previous visitors and occupants.

It is hoped that the renovated Cheboygan home will keep safe old memories while creating new and meaningful shared moments. The improvements will revitalize the house as a joyous living space, and they will be worth far more than the cost of their labor and materials.

House Remodel Overview

Multi-phased Project

Phase One

Autumn 2020 - Spring 2022

Kitchen, dining room, living room, bathroom

Renovation and re-modeling work of the Cheboygan House can be divided into three overlapping phases spanning multiple years. In the initial years, phase 1 of the renovation will target the shared living spaces comprising of the kitchen, front bathroom, dining room and living room. In these rooms, the renovations will address cosmetic as well necessary mechanical and structural updates -- like plumbing, electrical, lighting, doors, and windows. The mechanical updates will provide additional protection from extreme weather with the potential to freeze exposed plumbing. In the first phase of renovations, construction projects will begin in the late fall and complete by early spring. This will allow the entire house to be available for use and enjoyment during the late-spring, summer, and peak-fall color seasons.

It should be noted that throughout all phases of the project, as a room is refreshed, electrical lighting and outlet circuitry will be wired to a new utility room sub-panel. Eventually, the entire house will be moved off the existing master panel in the baby bedroom and re-wired to the utility room electrical panel. In the second and third phases of renovation, re-modeling projects will be structured to minimize power and water outages, so that the house may accomodate visitors year round.



Phase Two

Spring 2022 - Spring 2026

Guest bedrooms, master bedroom, deck, and landscaping

Beginning in the Spring of 2022, the second phase of the project will shift its attention to the exterior landscaping and to the bedrooms. This phase will span 2-5 years. Additional trees, shrubs, and flowers will be planted around the property. The exterior hardscape surrounding the house will be redone and a new deck will be added. The guest and baby bedrooms will receive modest, mostly cosmetic updates. The scope of work will be more compact in these two smaller bedrooms and, consequently, less disruptive to use of the house.

In the second renovation phase, the master bedroom will receive a more extensive update. The walls and ceiling will be stripped back to the stud framework. Insulation will be refreshed where needed to provide sound and temperature protection. Exposed plumbing will be covered. The room will be drywalled, trimmed and painted, and the carpeting will be replaced.



Phase Three

Date range - to be determined

Craft room/office, second bathroom, utility room, other

The third phase of the Cheboygan home renovation project will move to the craft room/office, back bathroom, utility room, garage, and roof. The house interior projects in this phase will update the walls, ceiling, built-ins, windows, and doors. Many of these updates will be cosmetic. However, the layouts of each of these rooms will be examined and improvements that might make these rooms more usable will be considered. When the time for replacement comes, various roofing options, including supplemental solar panels, will be investigated. Like the other phases, cost, budget, maintainability, and expected benefit will be important considerations in determining the final scope of the third phase of renovations.
