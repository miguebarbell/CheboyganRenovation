Living Room 2021-2022

The living room addition was built in the early-mid 1970's. Grandma and Grandpa Castle contracted Gordon Spies from Cheboygan, MI to add the gabled living room structure off the current dining room into the sloping ground in front of the house. In areas of the attic, you can see the roof structure of the original house built by Great-great Grandpa Frost and his neighbors, Mr. Hardy and Charlie LeDuc.

Gordon Spies laid the foundation and framed this first addition, set the windows and door, sided the exterior, and shingled the roof. Grandpa Castle finished the interior, including: electrical wiring and hook-up; laying insulation in the walls and ceiling; installing the wall paneling; trimming the windows, doors and thresholds; and installing carpet. Grandma Castle decorated the living room and sewed the window drapes.

Despite the extra space, the house was still a three-season cottage. A wood-burning stove was installed in the northeast corner of the living room to supplement the gas space heater in the dining room. While cozy, the stove was insufficient to properly heat the house when the nights went below freezing. Every year in early October, the plumbing was drained and the house was winterized until it could be re-opened in late May the following year.

The next addition, in the late 1970's, added the master bedroom, craft room and back bathroom. Hot water heating was installed by Werner's Plumbing. Insulation foam board was laid over the original house timbers and exterior paneling was attached. Grandpa Castle rolled out fiberglass insulation between the ceiling joists in the attic - a very hot and itchy job in the summertime. These improvements made the house liveable year-round, and Grandma and Grandpa Castle moved from Detroit to Cheboygan in 1981.

Living room interior North wall demolition

This is a photo of the North wall demolition and new window installation. This photo shows the interior paneling and floating ceiling removed. The plywood will stabilize the wall and prevent any slight shifting which would cause cracks in the drywall.
